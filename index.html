Positive Youth Development

How can we help adolescents reach their full potential?

Positive Youth Development (PYD) is an approach to youth people’s development that sees the best in every teenager. By recognizing that they have special skills that can help them reach their full potential, instead of focusing on their weaknesses, we can unlock their untapped potential and help them become amazing young people with bright futures.

Instead of simply trying to prevent bad behavior, PYD emphasizes engaging youth in positive activities and relationships that help them grow. When people in a child's community (including parents) create a positive environment where adolescents can be involved in productive, learning-focused activities, they are more likely to grow into healthy, capable, and responsible adults. At its core, PYD is all about nurturing the qualities we want to see in young people by giving them the opportunities they need to thrive.

The 5 C's of PYD

What does a thriving adolescent look like?

Research has revealed that adolescents are at their best when they embody a set of five key qualities. These qualities are known as the Five C's of PYD and include building competence, caring for others, being confident in their own self and abilities, connecting with others, and having integrity with a strong character. While each of these qualities is valuable on its own, when an adolescent is able to cultivate all of them, they are more likely to be able to meaningfully contribute to their communities in ways that support themselves and others.
